BMI Categories & Facts

Underweight (BMI < 18.5)

  • Can indicate malnutrition and a weakened immune system.
  • May result from genetics, illness, or eating disorders.
  • Increasing nutrient-rich foods is important for health.

Normal Weight (BMI 18.5–24.9)

  • Associated with the lowest risk of chronic diseases.
  • Maintain a balanced diet and regular exercise for good health.
  • Supports heart health, strong bones, and overall well-being.

Overweight (BMI 25–29.9)

  • Increases risk of heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.
  • Healthy lifestyle changes can help manage weight.
  • Risk increases as BMI rises.

Obese (BMI 30+)

  • Significantly increases risk of chronic diseases.
  • May require long-term changes in diet and activity.
  • The higher the BMI, the greater the health risks.
